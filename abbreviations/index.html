Abbreviations

auct.
auct. mult.
auct. nec
auct. non
auctorum means a taxon name was published by that author. auctorum multorum indicates the same for multiple authors. Converseley, auctorum non and auctorum nec mean a taxon name was not published by that author, indicating that a different author used the same taxon name for a different taxon, and that that different taxon is being discussed.
f.
forma (plural: formae), an intraspecific taxon below subspecies and variety.
f. n. g.
f. n. sp.
fauna nova genus (plural: faunae novae genera) and fauna nova species (plural: faunae novae species) mean that the taxa are new for the fauna of a specific area.
preocc.
preoccupied mean that the same taxon name was already in use for something else.
sp.
spec.
species
sp. n.
sp. nov.
spp. n.
spp. nov.
species nova (plural: species novae) means a taxon is published for the first time in that publication.
ssp.
subsp.
subspecies, an intraspecific taxon. Often used for groups with distinct geographical distributions.
var.
variety, an intraspecific taxon. Often used for when groups are not distributed in distinct areas.
